# What is the Eisenhower Matrix?

*Sharon Saline, Psy.D.*

*ADDitude Magazine, 2022*

The Eisenhower Matrix is a simple, effective decision-making tool for determining which tasks deserve our immediate action, our long-term attention, our delegation skills, and our circular bins.

It's human nature. When a fire sparks up, we drop everything to fight it. But in our ADHD lives, there are little fires everywhere. And we get so busy extinguishing flames sometimes that we can spend a whole day — maybe a week — spinning around and around in a mess of fire retardant. We're exhausted but have nothing to show for our time or energy.

If this sounds familiar, let me introduce you to the Eisenhower Matrix, a decision-making tool devised by the 34th President of the United States — a five-star general who accomplished a dizzying amount of important work in his two terms. Use the chart below to help determine which flare-ups could be delegated or ignored, and which ones actually deserve your precious attention.

---

## The Matrix

|                   | **Urgent**        | **Not Urgent**      |
|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Important**     | DO NOW            | PLAN                |
| **Not Important** | DELEGATE          | STOP                |

---

## Quadrant 1: Urgent + Important — DO NOW

**Drop Everything: Do These Tasks Right Now**

**Examples:**
- Client projects
- Team deadlines
- Family emergencies
- Emails requiring a response

**Tactics:**
- Use a timer to keep you focused and moving forward
- Avoid burnout by anticipating these tasks

---

## Quadrant 2: Not Urgent + Important — PLAN

**Carve Out Time: Plan and Prioritize These Long-Term Goals**

**Examples:**
- Exercise and nutrition
- Relationship maintenance
- Professional development
- Home projects

**Tactics:**
- Reserve regular blocks in your schedule for these tasks
- Tie these tasks to your life values

---

## Quadrant 3: Urgent + Not Important — DELEGATE

**Delegate These Interruptions: Find Someone Else to Carry This Weight**

**Examples:**
- Busywork, like paying bills
- Requests from colleagues in other departments
- Noncritical meetings
- Unscheduled phone calls

**Tactics:**
- Turn off phone notifications
- Check email only three times a day
- Practice saying no

---

## Quadrant 4: Not Urgent + Not Important — STOP

**Stop These Distractions: Call Yourself Out When You Slip Into Avoidance and Procrastination**

**Examples:**
- Mindlessly scrolling social media
- Online shopping
- Organizing but not processing papers
- Gaming or bingeing shows

**Tactics:**
- Use tools to track your screen use
- Use accountability partners
- Reward yourself for breaking these bad habits
